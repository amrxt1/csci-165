Why learn Linux? Benefits of learning Linux There are many reasons to learn Linux. Linux is a high-performance operating system based on UNIX. As a learner, you are bound to benefit from Linux. Let’s list the reasons to learn Linux below.


Click this to make above text bold.

  1. Interesting to learn Linux is interesting to learn. It will not stop surprising you when it comes to learning something “new.” If you have an inquisitive nature and a curious personality, you are going to love what it has to offer!
  2. Light on your pocket and saves money Linux is a free-to-use open-source operating system. This means that you do not have to pay anything for it. Unlike Windows or other paid operating system, there is no license fee. All you need to do is download Linux and get started. The only prerequisite is to have a machine that is capable of running Linux. It can even run on old computers. Apart from the operating system itself, you don’t have to pay for the applications. Linux ecosystem is big, and you can find the application(s) to do everything you want! If you use Linux, you may not have to buy any software.
  3. Developers heaven Developers love Linux. With Linux, they get a playground to try out new things. It supports all major programming language. Also, companies use Linux as the primary OS when it comes to software development and deployment. This also means that as a learner, you need to learn Linux for your professional career.
  4. Improve success at career As Linux is seen as an ideal OS for development, it also improves your chances of improving your career. At software development companies, it is common to have Linux as a necessary skill. If you become proficient in Linux, you are bound to get exposure as a developer.
  5. Teach Others Even in 2019, we have a huge demand for projects that add value to the learner 's experience. This is where you can get the chance to share your knowledge. On the Education Ecosystem, you get to explore the possibility of reaching the intermediate Linux learners. Our creators program lets you share and learn at the same time. You can get started by creating your very first Linux-based project.
